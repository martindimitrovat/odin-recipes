Jambalaya

Description

Jambalaya is a staple dish of c Cajun cuisine. There are many variants of it but the classic variant includes green bell pepper, onion and celery as its base also known as the "holy trinity". Other ingredients are andouille sausage, shrimp and chicken. In our recipe we will be using chorizo as a substitute for andouille, because many times it is not available. Actually any smoked sausage will do.

Ingredients

  • 500 g boneless skinless chicken thighs
  • 16 large shrimp
  • One chorizo
  • 3 bell peppers
  • A bunch of spring onions
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 500 - 750 ml of chicken broth
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 250 g long grain rice
  • 4 tsp Cajun spice or:
    • 1 pinch of cayenne pepper
    • 1 pinch of dried thyme
    • 1 pinch of garlic powder
    • 1 pinch of onion powder
    • 1 pinch of smoked paprika powder
    • 1 pinch of paprika powder
  • salt
  • pepper
  • parsley

Steps

  1. Cut the chicken thigh meat into even small cubes. Salt and pepper both sides and put a little flour on them. Peel the shrimp if needed and dry them with paper towels. Cut the spring onions into rings. Dice the bell peppers. Cut up the chorizo. Get your broth and Cajun spice ready.
  2. In a large pot or wok fry the shrimp and chicken pieces and set aside.
  3. Then put the chorizo into the pot and start frying on low to medium heat until it releases all its juices, then put the spices in and make them fragrant but don't let them burn. Get your tomato paste and add it. Then add the spring onions and bell peppers. Mix well. Add the long grain rice and add the broth.
  4. Let it cook for 25 min at medium to low heat and stir it regularly to avoid burning the rice.
  5. Re add the chicken and shrimp cook for another 10 min at low heat.
  6. Plate and add some parsley on top for garnish.
